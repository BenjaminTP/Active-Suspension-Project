# Active Suspension Project Phase 1

### Purpose
One day, as I was mindlessly scrolling on my phone, I came across a video of a car seemingly levitating above a road full of bumps. It was absolutely mesmerizing to look at, so I decided to build it myself. Since I do not have the budget to build out a full car, I decided to build out a quarter-car suspension system. 

What I initially thought would be a simple project quickly turned into a project full of vibrations, dynamics, differential equations, fourier series, and topics I had never learned about before. Although this project immediately seemed impossible, I persevered through math and physics that I will not encounter until my second or third year of engineering.

I truly wanted to learn everything about this mesmerizing active suspension system, so I put in a lot of work to understand as much as possible. All the work I present here is my own, and not AI generated, except for one cool visualization at the end which I clearly have labeled as not my own work.

I learned a ton from this project, and I wish to present my full end-to-end engineering process below.

### Project Scopes
I decided to break this project up into two phases: phase 1 would consist of the initial research, learning, and simulations on a quarter car with real-world specs, while phase 2 would physically build out the real-world model based off of the work done in phase 1.
